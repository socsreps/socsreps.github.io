National Student Survey Results 2020

By Garry Clawson

June 24, 2020

Every year the Office for Students compiles a National Student Survey (NSS) which is completed by all final year students. This survey consists of 27 questions (the 2020 survey questions are linked here) ranging from the teaching on your course to the learning resources available. Completing the survey is voluntary but the more students who complete it the better as it enables a more representative picture of the course as a whole. It is important to look at the survey results because they inform the SoCS leadership of areas that are doing well and what areas can do better. This will directly effect you as a student.

Over 150 Computer Science students and over 25 Games Computing students completed the survey in 2020, but not many will take a look at the results. Perhaps even fewer current year 1 and 2 students would do this. This blog hopes to correct that and provide this information without you having to spend hours trying to find it. Due to the quantity of questions this blog will be split into two sections 1) Computer Science and, 2) Games Computing. Links to the actual NSS survey results are below

  • Computer Science - NSS Results

  • Games Computing - NSS Results

I will not attempt to interpret the results because everyone's university experience is specific to them but what I will do is ask that no matter what year you are in, if you are not having the experience you want then please reach out and let us know. This can be done through the Contact Us section or Feedback Form. No concern or idea is too small.
